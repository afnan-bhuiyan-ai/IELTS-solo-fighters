Your B2 → 7.5+ roadmap
Phase 1 — Foundations (weeks 1–4) Now
Diagnose weak spots with a full mock test. Learn all question types per module. Build 10 new academic words/day. Focus on grammar accuracy (complex sentences, passive voice).
Phase 2 — Skills sharpening (weeks 5–10)
Module-by-module deep practice. Write 2 Task 2 essays/week with structure review. Do 2 full Listening tests/day. Start timing yourself strictly. Expand collocations and paraphrasing.
Phase 3 — Mock & refine (weeks 11–14)
One full mock test per week under real conditions. Fix recurring error patterns. Speaking practice daily with recording. No new topics — consolidate and sharpen.
Weekly focus schedule
Each day = 60–90 min. Rotate: Listening · Reading · Writing · Speaking · Vocab review
🎧
Listening
Easiest to improve fast
Read all questions before audio starts — predict the answer type
Watch for distractors — speakers often say wrong answer first, then correct it
Strictly follow word limits: "no more than two words" means exactly that
Practice British, Australian & NZ accents (BBC, E2 IELTS YouTube)
Do 2 full tests/day in Phase 2, review transcripts after every test
Record 10 phrases per section in 3–5 word chunks
📖
Reading
Information-finding, not reading
Read questions first → scan passage for answers (never read everything first)
Spot capital letters, numbers, and italics — they're often direct answers
True/False/Not Given: "Not Given" means info is absent, not wrong
Spend 18–20 min on easier passages, save time for harder ones
Practice with The Guardian, BBC, The Economist weekly
Master synonyms — answers use different words than the passage
✍️
Writing
Needs most time investment
Task 1: 20 min, 150+ words — describe trends, never give opinions
Task 2: 40 min, 270–280 words — clear thesis + 2 body paras + conclusion
Paraphrase the question in your intro — shows vocabulary range immediately
Use collocations: "fundamental reason," "significant impact," "strongly argue"
Avoid idioms in essays — they're informal. Use phrasal verbs carefully instead
Plan for 5 min before writing — structure prevents incoherence
🗣️
Speaking
Confidence + fluency wins
Part 1: natural, relaxed short answers. Don't over-explain.
Part 2: use your 1-min prep — jot 3 key points, tell a story
Part 3: give opinions with reasons + examples: "I think… because… for example…"
Use connectors: "As a result," "On the other hand," "That being said"
Record yourself daily — review for filler words (um, uh) and pacing
Think in English. Watch 4 speaking test videos/week on YouTube (E2 IELTS)
Scoring criteria (what examiners actually check)
Writing scored on:
Task achievement · Coherence & cohesion · Lexical resource · Grammatical range & accuracy
Speaking scored on:
Fluency & coherence · Lexical resource · Grammatical range & accuracy · Pronunciation
